Both Abigail and Trump also used scapegoating to great effect. In the play, Abigail is cornered by Hale’s questions. To escape being called a witch, she blames it on Tituba, later and long list of other names as well: “I saw Goody Sibber with the Devil!.... I saw Goody Booth with the Devil!”(Miller 595-596). In that quote, it is seen that Abigail uses scapegoating to remove the blame for any wrongdoing of herself and onto other members of society, thus leaving her name clean. Trump does the exact same thing. He has no shortage of scandals: Russian collusion, tax returns, Payouts to mistresses, etc. However, he always shifts the blame everytime he is asked about one of them. One of his most popular moves on the campaign trail was blaming Hillary Clinton for letting American soldiers die during Benghazi. An example of him using Benghazi to scapegoat Clinton is: “In a June 22 speech, Trump said Clinton's decisions as secretary of state "spread death, destruction and terrorism everywhere she touched. Among the victims was our late Ambassador Chris Stevens. I mean what she did with him was absolutely horrible. He was left helpless to die as Hillary Clinton soundly slept in her bed. That's right. When the phone rang, as per the commercial, at 3:00 in the morning, Hillary Clinton was sleeping."(Carrol). This is just one of the many ways that Trump used scapegoating to keep his name clean with his base. Other examples include “Uranium One” and Clinton’s use of a private email server for government work. Clearly, both Abigail and Trump used scapegoating to shift any blame wrongdoing, and this can be seen as a form of fear weaponization as well. By scapegoating someone, you are essentially making the masses fear their integrity and character, thus further proving that the mob mentality great susceptibility to fear is an ambitious man’s greatest tool.
