"Trump shows himself as the protector of America against all the evil that those cities are protecting. Abigail did the same thing in The Crucible"

In The Crucible Abigail Williams gains power by wielding fear, and so did Donald Trump in 2016. In the play, Abigail is just a simple girl in a Puritan society. In Salem, girls were sidelined and had almost no power. They could not speak up during church or against men in general. But despite that, in the winter of 1692, Abigail Williams controlled who lived and died, and the entire town stopped to hear her words. How was this? Abigail offered protection against what they all feared, witches. By telling the masses that she had been approached by the devil, and knew who in town were the devil’s agents, she was able to override all better judgement and get an irrational following. This trance can be seen when Elizabeth says that “Abigail brings the other girls into the court, and where she walks the crowd will part like the seas or Israel. And folks are brought before them, and if they scream and howl and fall to the floor-the person’s clapped in the jail for bewitchin’ them.” (Miller 604). It can be seen that due to the fear of witchcraft, Abigail was able to gain the power to get anyone jailed, a power we will see her misuse. The election of Donald Trump shows a similar effect. Trump had zero knowledge of politics and was significantly less qualified than his opponent. He broke almost every norm of a political candidate. He insulted a gold star family, he disparaged recognized war heroes, and he was accused of multiple sexual assaults mere weeks before the election. Yet through it, all his base stayed loyal, and with every setback, grew almost more fanatical. This was because he had entranced them by convincing them he could protect them from the dangers of liberal America. He said that Democrats were coming for their guns, they wanted to globalize and take away factory jobs, and they wanted open borders that led to Latino gangs like MS-13. Every rally was a fear-party painting a surprisingly bleak picture of the world’s richest country and last superpower. He has continued to do this as President, one example being the crackdown on sanctuary cities. Trump justified ICE’s crackdown by stating: “To hear the president tell it -- along with a like-minded group of almost uniformly Republican officials, politicians and law enforcement -- sanctuary cities are intent on unleashing a crime wave on America. They are doing so, Mr Trump and his claque asserted, by safeguarding murderers, rapists and other bloodthirsty felons on what amounts to a virtual underground railroad traversed by illegal immigrants between local jails and vulnerable communities.”(Trump convenes). Again, in the sanctuary city issue, Trump shows himself as the protector of America against all the evil that those cities are protecting. Abigail did the same thing in The Crucible, by making herself the only connection that people had with the supernatural world, which they so feared, she created herself into the protector against witchcraft. Both these are examples of textbook fear-mongering and has countless examples throughout history. They show how the mob mentality is the key weakness that society has, and if you can exploit it, nothing stops your rapid rise to power.
